## Purpose

This worksheet helps attendees identify who will act on their behalf if they can't. It reduces family chaos by clarifying roles and responsibilities before a crisis occurs.

---

## Emotional Outcomes

### Before State
**Emotional State:** Discomfort
- Uncomfortable thinking about incapacity
- Unclear who should act
- Haven't had conversations with family
- Worried about burdening others

### After State
**Emotional State:** Responsible confidence
- Clear about who will act
- Backups identified
- Sense of responsibility fulfilled
- Confidence that family knows what to do

---

## Worksheet Structure

### Section 1: Financial Agent

**Prompts:**
- "If I can't act, my financial agent is: _________"
- "Backup: _________"

**Purpose:** Identify who handles financial decisions if incapacitated

**Guidance:** This is the person named in Financial Power of Attorney. Should be someone trustworthy, organized, and capable of managing finances.

### Section 2: Healthcare Agent

**Prompts:**
- "My healthcare agent is: _________"
- "Backup: _________"

**Purpose:** Identify who makes healthcare decisions if incapacitated

**Guidance:** This is the person named in Healthcare Power of Attorney/Healthcare Directive. Should be someone who understands your values and can make difficult medical decisions.

### Section 3: Executor / Successor Trustee

**Prompts:**
- "Executor / successor trustee candidates: _________"
- "Backup: _________"

**Purpose:** Identify who handles estate administration after death

**Guidance:** 
- **Executor:** Handles will administration (if no trust)
- **Successor Trustee:** Takes over trust management (if trust exists)
- Should be someone organized, trustworthy, and capable of handling legal/financial tasks

### Section 4: Notes

**Prompt:** "Why I chose them" (1 sentence)

**Purpose:** Document reasoning for choices (helps with future review and family understanding)

**Guidance:** Brief explanation of why each person was chosen. Examples: "Organized and trustworthy," "Understands my values," "Lives nearby," "Has financial experience."

## Usage Instructions

### For Attendees
1. Start with primary choices (don't worry about backups yet)
2. Think about who you trust and who is capable
3. Consider location, availability, and willingness
4. Write brief notes about why you chose them
5. Backups can be added later if needed

### For Facilitators
- Emphasize that backups are optional initially
- Remind them they can change these later
- Encourage thinking about capability, not just relationships
- This is about clarity, not perfection

---

## Common Questions

**Q: What if I don't have someone I trust?**
A: That's okay. You can leave it blank and revisit later. Some people choose professional trustees or attorneys.

**Q: Can the same person be multiple roles?**
A: Yes, but consider the workload. Having backups is important.

**Q: What if my chosen person doesn't want to do it?**
A: Have a conversation first. It's important they're willing. If not, choose someone else.

**Q: Do I need backups?**
A: It's recommended but not required. Start with primary choices.
