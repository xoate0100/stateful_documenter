# Worksheet 1 — Legacy Snapshot

**Source:** Lines 278-304 of `evidence/source_material.md`  
**Purpose:** Reduce overwhelm; establish baseline  
**Length:** 1 page  
**Time Estimate:** 10-minute start

---

## Purpose

This worksheet helps attendees establish a baseline of what they have, where it is, and who knows about it. It reduces overwhelm by breaking down the "big picture" into manageable pieces.

---

## Emotional Outcomes

### Before State
**Emotional State:** Uncertain, scattered
- Don't know what they have
- Don't know where documents are
- Don't know who knows what
- Overwhelmed by the scope

### After State
**Emotional State:** Oriented, relieved
- Clear picture of what exists
- Know where things are located
- Know who has access/knowledge
- Sense of orientation and control

---

## Worksheet Structure

### Section 1: Documents I Have

Checkboxes for:
- ☐ Will
- ☐ Trust
- ☐ POA (Fin) - Financial Power of Attorney
- ☐ POA (Health) - Healthcare Power of Attorney
- ☐ Healthcare directive

**Purpose:** Quick inventory of existing documents

### Section 2: Where Stored

**Prompt:** "Where stored (physical + digital): __________________"

**Purpose:** Document location (both physical and digital copies)

**Guidance:** Include both physical location (safe, filing cabinet, attorney's office) and digital location (cloud storage, password manager, etc.)

### Section 3: Who Knows Where They Are

**Prompt:** "Who knows where they are: __________________"

**Purpose:** Identify who has access or knowledge of document locations

**Guidance:** List family members, attorney, financial advisor, or other trusted individuals who know where documents are stored.

### Section 4: Immediate Next Step

**Prompt:** "Immediate next step (choose one):"

Options:
- ☐ Locate documents
- ☐ Name decision makers
- ☐ Check beneficiaries
- ☐ Start digital inventory

**Purpose:** Provide clear, actionable next step to reduce paralysis

**Guidance:** Choose ONE step to focus on. This prevents overwhelm and creates momentum.

## Usage Instructions

### For Attendees
1. Check off documents you have
2. Write where they're stored (physical + digital)
3. List who knows where they are
4. Choose ONE immediate next step
5. Don't worry about completing everything—this is a start

### For Facilitators
- Emphasize "10-minute start" framing
- Remind them they can choose one next step
- Don't create pressure to complete everything
- This is about orientation, not perfection
