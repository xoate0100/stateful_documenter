Business or personal recovery monthly process of first year
Quarter or annual (loan portion)
1. Day 1-Client sends premium into insurance company for the quarter or annual.
2. Day 2- Client borrows 92% of cash value as policy loan and deposits into checking account, and pays the expenses as normal.


Quarter or annual 2(repayment portion)
1. Day 1- Client repays loan of previous quarter or annual with cash flow that was being used to pay expenses, plus 5% covers interest to insurance company account.  Keep in mind you are still getting the dividend growth in your policy as well with no interruption.
2. Client borrows money back out of policy to pay expenses as usual.




By the 6th year you can drop the policy premium down to the base premium if needed and continue to borrow out the full amount needed for expenses:
Cash value grows with interest and dividends and deposits each month. Illustrated on insurance software and borrowing calculator)
The annual premium will come from the normal flow of income each year through the process described above.
