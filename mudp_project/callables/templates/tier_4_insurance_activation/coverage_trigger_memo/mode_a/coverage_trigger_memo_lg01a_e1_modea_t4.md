**Trigger Analysis:**

- **Bodily Injury:** The incident causes bodily injury (emotional distress, psychological harm)

#### 2. Defense Obligation Trigger

**Trigger Analysis:**
- **Suit or Claim:** Potential claims or suits may be filed
- **Coverage Potential:** Coverage may apply to potential claims
- **Defense Duty:** Carrier has duty to defend if coverage potentially applies

**Defense Obligation:**
- Carrier must provide defense if coverage potentially applies
- Defense obligation is broader than indemnity obligation
- Defense must be provided even if coverage is uncertain

#### 3. Additional Coverage Triggers

**Other Potential Triggers:**
- **Umbrella Coverage:** If primary coverage is exhausted
- **Excess Coverage:** If claims exceed primary limits
- **Cyber Liability:** If policy includes cyber coverage
- **Other Coverage:** Other applicable coverage provisions

### Defense Obligations

**Duty to Defend:**
Under applicable insurance law, the carrier has a duty to defend if:
- The complaint alleges facts that potentially fall within coverage
- Coverage potentially applies to the claims
- No clear exclusions apply

**Defense Obligations Include:**
- Providing legal defense
- Paying defense costs
- Retaining defense counsel
- Investigating claims
- Defending against claims

**Defense vs. Indemnity:**
- **Defense:** Broader obligation, applies if coverage potentially applies
- **Indemnity:** Narrower obligation, applies only if coverage actually applies
