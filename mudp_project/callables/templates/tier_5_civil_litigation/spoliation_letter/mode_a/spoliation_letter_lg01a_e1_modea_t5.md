### Preservation Obligations

**Legal Obligations:**
Under applicable law, your client has a legal obligation to:
- **Preserve All Evidence:** Preserve all evidence related to this matter
- **Prevent Destruction:** Prevent destruction, alteration, or modification of evidence
- **Maintain Original Form:** Maintain evidence in its original form
- **Preserve Metadata:** Preserve all metadata and technical information
- **Document Preservation:** Document all preservation actions taken

**Duty to Preserve:**
- The duty to preserve evidence arises when litigation is reasonably anticipated
- The duty continues throughout the litigation
- The duty applies to all parties and their agents
- The duty applies to all relevant evidence

### Spoliation Consequences

**Legal Consequences:**
Destruction, alteration, or spoliation of evidence may result in:

1. **Adverse Inference Instructions:**
   - Court may instruct jury to infer that destroyed evidence was unfavorable
   - Adverse inferences can be devastating to a case
   - May result in liability findings

2. **Monetary Sanctions:**
   - Court may impose monetary penalties
   - May include costs and attorneys' fees
   - May include punitive sanctions

3. **Contempt of Court:**
   - Court may hold party in contempt
   - May result in fines or imprisonment
   - Serious consequences for willful destruction

4. **Case Dispositive Sanctions:**
   - Court may dismiss claims or defenses
   - Court may enter default judgment
   - Most severe sanction available

5. **Criminal Penalties:**
   - Destruction of evidence may be a crime
   - May result in criminal prosecution
   - Serious criminal consequences

**Warning:**
**ANY destruction, alteration, or spoliation of evidence may result in severe legal sanctions, including adverse inference instructions, monetary penalties, contempt of court, case dispositive sanctions, and criminal penalties.**

### Specific Warnings

**Specific Warnings:**

1. **Do Not Delete:** Do not delete any files, documents, or records
2. **Do Not Modify:** Do not modify, alter, or change any evidence
3. **Do Not Destroy:** Do not destroy any devices, documents, or evidence
4. **Do Not Wipe:** Do not wipe, format, or reset any devices
5. **Do Not Dispose:** Do not dispose of any evidence
6. **Do Not Transfer:** Do not transfer or move evidence without authorization
7. **Do Not Access:** Do not access evidence in a way that modifies it
8. **Preserve Everything:** Preserve everything that may be relevant

**Technical Warnings:**

- Do not defragment hard drives

- Do not delete emails, messages, or communications
