## Overview

The Crisis Playbook provides step-by-step procedures for responding to incidents involving unauthorized use of children's digital likeness, AI-generated sexualized content, or other digital personhood violations. This playbook ensures fast, clean resolution with maximum protection for your child.

**Principle:** Activate pre-built systems, deploy prepared responses, use established leverage, maintain professional tone, focus on resolution.

---

## Immediate Triage Checklist

**When an incident is discovered, immediately:**

1. **Secure Copies**
   - Download/screenshot all instances of the image
   - Document all URLs where image appears
   - Capture metadata if available
   - Save to secure, private location

2. **Document URLs**
   - List all platforms/apps where image appears
   - Record exact URLs
   - Screenshot each instance
   - Note timestamps

3. **Verify Hash Match**
   - Run hash comparison against registry
   - Check perceptual hash similarity
   - Verify embedding vector match (if available)
   - Document match percentage

4. **Contain Distribution**
   - Assess scope of distribution
   - Identify all platforms involved
   - Note any sharing/forwarding
   - Document distribution timeline

**Time Target:** Complete within 1 hour of discovery

## Response Levels

The playbook uses a tiered response system aligned with the Enforcement Tiers. Each level represents a different escalation point and response strategy.

---

## Level 1 Response: Parents Only

**Trigger:** Initial incident discovery, soft resolution attempt

**Objective:** Resolve issue through direct parent-to-parent communication

### Steps

1. **Prepare Response Packet**
   - Retrieve pre-written Parent Notification Letter
   - Customize with specific incident details
   - Include legal standing documentation
   - Attach evidence (hashes, registry entries)

2. **Contact Parents**
   - Send Parent Notification Letter
   - Maintain calm, factual, non-accusatory tone
   - Be clear about legal standing
   - Request immediate action

3. **Document Communication**
   - Record all communications
   - Timestamp all interactions
   - Save all correspondence
   - Note response times

4. **Monitor Response**
   - Track response time
   - Assess cooperation level
   - Document any resistance
   - Evaluate resolution progress

### Expected Outcome

- Image removed within 24-48 hours
- All copies deleted
- Accounts deactivated
- Apology/acknowledgment
- Cooperation with resolution

### Success Indicators

- ✅ Immediate acknowledgment
- ✅ Cooperation demonstrated
- ✅ Image removed quickly
- ✅ All copies deleted
- ✅ Issue resolved

### Failure Triggers (Escalate to Level 2)

- ❌ No response within 24 hours
- ❌ Refusal to cooperate
- ❌ Defensive/combative response
- ❌ Continued distribution
- ❌ Denial of responsibility

**Timeline:** 24-48 hours for resolution

---

## Level 2 Response: School Activation

**Trigger:** Level 1 fails OR incident involves school context

**Objective:** Activate school district compliance framework

### Steps

1. **Activate School District Notice**
   - Send pre-written School District Notice
   - Reference Title IX coordinator
   - Contact district legal
   - Include mandatory reporting requirements

2. **Trigger Title IX Process**
   - File Title IX complaint
   - Reference sexual harassment policy
   - Request immediate investigation
   - Demand protective measures

3. **Activate Mandatory Reporting**
   - Ensure school follows mandatory reporting protocols
   - Document all reporting actions
   - Verify compliance
   - Track response

4. **Demand Student Safety Plan**
   - Request immediate safety measures
   - Demand disciplinary action
   - Require image removal
   - Insist on protection protocols

5. **Document All Actions**
   - Record school responses
   - Timestamp all communications
   - Document compliance/non-compliance
   - Maintain audit trail

### Expected Outcome

- School takes immediate action
- Title IX investigation initiated
- District legal involved
- Mandatory reporting completed
- Student safety measures implemented
- Disciplinary action taken

### Success Indicators

- ✅ School takes disciplinary action
- ✅ Title IX process initiated
- ✅ District legal review
- ✅ Mandatory reporting completed
- ✅ Student safety measures implemented

### Failure Triggers (Escalate to Level 4)

- ❌ School delays or downplays
- ❌ Failure to follow mandatory reporting
- ❌ Inadequate disciplinary action
- ❌ Lack of district legal involvement

**Timeline:** Immediate (within hours)

---

## Level 3 Response: Platform Removal

**Trigger:** Content on platforms/apps, Level 1-2 insufficient

**Objective:** Force immediate platform removal through legal pressure

### Steps

1. **Prepare Platform Removal Packet**
   - Assemble Rapid Response Legal Packet
   - Include CSAM declaration
   - Add Right of Publicity violation
   - Include tortious interference
   - Add defamation notice
   - Include copyright claim (DMCA)
   - Add biometric misuse claim

2. **Send Emergency Takedown Request**
   - Submit to platform immediately
   - Use multiple legal regimes
   - Reference CSAM statutes
   - Demand immediate removal

3. **Send Evidence Preservation Notice**
   - Demand log retention
   - Require IP address preservation
   - Request timestamp documentation
   - Insist on evidence preservation

4. **Monitor Platform Response**
   - Track removal time
   - Verify content removal
   - Confirm account suspension
   - Document compliance

### Expected Outcome

- Content removed immediately (within hours)
- Account suspended/deleted
- Platform compliance
- Evidence preserved
- No further distribution

### Success Indicators

- ✅ Content removed within hours
- ✅ Account suspended/deleted
- ✅ Platform compliance
- ✅ Evidence preserved
- ✅ No further distribution

### Failure Triggers (Escalate to Level 4)

- ❌ Platform delays removal
- ❌ Inadequate response
- ❌ Continued availability
- ❌ Failure to preserve evidence

**Timeline:** Immediate (platforms act fast on CSAM)

---

## Level 4 Response: Insurance Activation

**Trigger:** Levels 1-3 insufficient OR concurrent with Level 2-3

**Objective:** Activate insurance carriers to force resolution

### Steps

1. **Prepare Insurance Incident Report**
   - Complete incident report template
   - Document liability exposure
   - Reference coverage triggers
   - Include legal basis

2. **Notify All Relevant Carriers**
   - School district's liability carrier
   - Parents' homeowner/umbrella coverage
   - Platform cyber-liability (if applicable)
   - Any other relevant policies

3. **Document Coverage Triggers**
   - Defamation coverage
   - Personal injury coverage
   - Cyber liability coverage
   - Educational liability coverage
   - Umbrella policy coverage

4. **Demand Immediate Remediation**
   - Request carrier intervention
   - Demand fast resolution
   - Insist on cooperation
   - Require evidence preservation

### Expected Outcome

- Insurance carrier involvement
- Immediate remediation push
- Coverage confirmed
- Legal cost support
- Forced cooperation from all parties

### Success Indicators

- ✅ Insurance carrier involvement
- ✅ Immediate remediation push
- ✅ Coverage confirmed
- ✅ Legal cost support
- ✅ Forced cooperation

### Failure Triggers (Escalate to Level 5)

- ❌ Coverage denial
- ❌ Carrier delays
- ❌ Inadequate coverage
- ❌ Failure to trigger policies

**Timeline:** Immediate (carriers act fast on liability)

---

## Level 5 Response: Legal Counsel Involvement

**Trigger:** Levels 1-4 insufficient, ongoing harm, or egregious conduct

**Objective:** Formal legal action with maximum leverage

### Steps

1. **Engage Legal Counsel**
   - Contact attorney immediately
   - Provide complete documentation
   - Share all evidence
   - Brief on legal position

2. **Prepare Formal Legal Demands**
   - Draft demand letters
   - Cite all causes of action
   - Quantify damages
   - Set deadlines

3. **File Legal Actions (If Necessary)**
   - File lawsuit if required
   - Seek injunctions
   - Demand damages
   - Request attorney's fees

4. **Pursue All Available Remedies**
   - Statutory damages
   - Punitive damages
   - Injunctions
   - Attorney's fees
   - Economic harm compensation

### Expected Outcome

- Formal legal action (if necessary)
- Strong legal position
- Multiple causes of action
- Quantifiable damages
- Maximum leverage
- Settlement negotiations
- Resolution achieved

### Success Indicators

- ✅ Legal counsel engaged
- ✅ Formal demands sent
- ✅ Strong legal position
- ✅ Settlement negotiations
- ✅ Resolution achieved

### Failure Triggers (Escalate to Level 6)

- ❌ Ongoing harm
- ❌ Refusal to settle
- ❌ Egregious conduct
- ❌ Inadequate resolution

**Timeline:** Weeks to months (litigation process)

---

## Level 6 Response: Criminal Pathways

**Trigger:** Egregious conduct, ongoing harm, or mandatory reporting requirements

**Objective:** Criminal investigation and prosecution (if warranted)

### Steps

1. **Complete Mandatory Reporting**
   - File NCMEC report (if required)
   - Complete all mandatory reporting
   - Document all actions
   - Maintain records

2. **Notify Law Enforcement**
   - Contact appropriate agencies
   - Provide evidence
   - File criminal complaint (if warranted)
   - Cooperate with investigation

3. **Support Criminal Investigation**
   - Provide all evidence
   - Assist with investigation
   - Testify if required
   - Support prosecution

**Note:** This tier includes ONLY informational sheets — not threats, just facts. Used only when necessary for public safety or when mandatory reporting is required by law.

**Timeline:** Ongoing (criminal process)

## Post-Resolution Follow-Up Procedures

**After issue is resolved, complete:**

1. **Trauma-Informed Conversation with Child**
   - Age-appropriate discussion
   - Address emotional impact
   - Provide support resources
   - Monitor ongoing well-being

2. **Documentation**
   - Complete incident report
   - Update all records
   - File all documentation
   - Maintain complete file

3. **Update Provenance Ledger**
   - Add incident to registry
   - Document resolution
   - Record all actions taken
   - Update hash records

4. **Update School Protections**
   - Review school policies
   - Update protection measures
   - Strengthen safeguards
   - Document improvements

5. **Review and Improve**
   - Assess response effectiveness
   - Identify improvements
   - Update playbook
   - Strengthen systems

## Decision Framework

### When to Escalate

Escalate to next level if:
- Previous level fails to resolve
- Ongoing harm continues
- No cooperation received
- Legal requirements mandate
- Public safety at risk

### Parallel Activation

Multiple levels can be activated simultaneously:
- Level 2 (School) + Level 4 (Insurance)
- Level 3 (Platform) + Level 4 (Insurance)
- All levels if necessary

### Professional Approach

**Always:**
- Maintain professional tone
- Document everything
- Follow legal procedures
- Focus on resolution
- Protect the child

**Never:**
- Make threats
- Escalate unnecessarily
- Act out of emotion
- Violate legal procedures
- Compromise child's safety
