# MUDP Drafting Rules

**Source:** SRD_DSKB.yaml  
**Purpose:** Formalize drafting standards for all pre-generated templates  
**Status:** Core System Component

---

## Overview

All pre-generated templates must follow these drafting rules to ensure:
- Consistent, professional tone
- Legal soundness
- Appropriate language strength
- Required elements included
- Prohibited language avoided

---

## Tone

**Required Tone:** `crisis_management_professional`

### Characteristics

- Calm and measured
- Factual and precise
- Professional and authoritative
- Not accusatory or inflammatory
- Clear and direct
- Respectful but firm

### Examples

✅ **Good:**
- "We have a credible concern that..."
- "This may trigger obligations under..."
- "We request immediate action to..."

❌ **Bad:**
- "You are guilty of..."
- "This is clearly illegal..."
- "We will destroy you..."

---

## Structure

### Required Elements

1. **Headings and Numbered Sections**
   - Clear document structure
   - Easy navigation
   - Professional appearance

2. **One Legal Assertion Per Paragraph**
   - Clear separation of claims
   - Easy to understand
   - Professional structure

3. **Separate Facts from Conclusions**
   - Facts stated clearly
   - Conclusions drawn separately
   - Logical flow

### Example Structure

```
1. FACTS
   1.1. Incident Description
   1.2. Evidence Available
   1.3. Timeline

2. LEGAL FRAMEWORK
   2.1. Applicable Laws
   2.2. Legal Obligations

3. DEMANDS
   3.1. Immediate Actions
   3.2. Preservation Requirements

4. BOILERPLATE
   4.1. Jurisdiction Disclaimer
   4.2. Non-Waiver of Rights
   4.3. Preservation Notice
   4.4. Good Faith Statement
   4.5. Escalation Reservation
```

## Mandatory Boilerplate Blocks

All templates must include these boilerplate sections:

### 1. Jurisdiction Disclaimer

**Purpose:** Clarify jurisdiction and legal framework

**Mode A (Agnostic):**
```
This notice is provided under applicable federal and state laws
governing child protection, privacy, and digital personhood rights.
Specific legal frameworks may vary by jurisdiction.
```

**Mode B (Specific):**
```
This notice is provided under [specific statutes] and applicable
federal and state laws governing child protection, privacy, and
digital personhood rights in [jurisdiction].
```

### 2. Non-Waiver of Rights

**Purpose:** Preserve all legal rights

```
Nothing in this communication constitutes a waiver of any legal
rights, claims, or remedies available under applicable law. All
rights are expressly reserved.
```

### 3. Preservation of Evidence Notice

**Purpose:** Create preservation obligations

```
You are hereby notified of your obligation to preserve all evidence
related to this matter, including but not limited to [specific
evidence types]. Destruction, alteration, or spoliation of evidence
may result in legal sanctions.
```

### 4. Good Faith Resolution Statement

**Purpose:** Demonstrate good faith

```
This communication is made in good faith with the intent to resolve
this matter promptly and appropriately. We are open to constructive
dialogue and reasonable resolution.
```

### 5. Reservation of Escalation Rights

**Purpose:** Preserve escalation options

```
This communication does not limit or waive our right to pursue any
and all available legal remedies, including but not limited to
[applicable remedies], should this matter not be resolved promptly
and appropriately.
```

## Prohibited Language

The following language is **prohibited** in all templates:

### Absolute Statements (Unless Mode B with Verified Citations)

- ❌ "we will prosecute"
- ❌ "you are guilty"
- ❌ "this is definitively illegal"
- ❌ "you have committed a crime"
- ❌ "this is clearly illegal"

**Exception:** Mode B templates with verified citations and supporting facts may use stronger language, but must still avoid criminal guilt assertions.

### Inflammatory Language

- ❌ Threats
- ❌ Personal attacks
- ❌ Emotional language
- ❌ Exaggerated claims
- ❌ Unprofessional tone

### Unsupported Claims

- ❌ Claims without evidence
- ❌ Speculative damages
- ❌ Unverified facts
- ❌ Overstated legal positions

---

## Preferred Language

Use these phrases to maintain professional tone while asserting legal positions:

### Credible Concerns

- ✅ "credible concern indicates"
- ✅ "this raises serious concerns"
- ✅ "we have a credible basis to believe"

### Conditional Framing

- ✅ "may trigger"
- ✅ "potentially applicable"
- ✅ "could constitute"
- ✅ "appears to violate"

### Action Requests

- ✅ "request immediate containment, removal, and preservation"
- ✅ "demand immediate action"
- ✅ "require prompt response"
- ✅ "request cooperation"

### Legal Framework References

- ✅ "under applicable [legal framework]"
- ✅ "pursuant to [legal framework]"
- ✅ "consistent with [legal framework]"
- ✅ "in accordance with [legal framework]"

## Language Strength by Evidence Class

### E1/E2 (Direct/Platform Confirmations)

**Strong Language Allowed:**
- "We have direct evidence that..."
- "The evidence demonstrates..."
- "This definitively shows..."
- "Official records confirm..."

### E3 (Derived Similarity)

**Moderate Language Required:**
- "Our analysis indicates similarity..."
- "Technical evidence suggests..."
- "Hash comparison shows..."
- "This appears to be derived from..."

**Prohibited:**
- Actor identification without correlating evidence
- Definitive attribution claims

### E4 (Testimonial)

**Moderate Language Required:**
- "Reports indicate..."
- "Witnesses state..."
- "According to reports..."
- "It has been reported that..."

### E5 (Contextual)

**Weak Language Required:**
- "Patterns suggest..."
- "Timeline indicates..."
- "Circumstantial evidence points to..."
- "This may indicate..."

## Language by Sexualization Level

### Explicit Minor Sexual Depiction (LG_01A)

**Required:**
- High-risk language
- "may trigger" framing
- "potentially applicable" language
- Avoid criminal guilt assertions
- Preserve reporting pathways

**Example:**
```
This content may trigger obligations under federal child sexual
exploitation frameworks. We have a credible concern that this
content potentially violates applicable laws.
```

### Sexualized Non-Explicit (LG_01B)

**Required:**
- Civil/school framework language
- Strong assertion of civil claims
- Criminal frameworks conditional only

**Example:**
```
This content appears to violate civil privacy and defamation
doctrines. This may also trigger Title IX obligations if school
context applies.
```

### Age Ambiguous (LG_01C)

**Required:**
- Conservative language
- High-risk treatment
- Preserve all options
- Pending clarification note

**Example:**
```
Given the age ambiguity, we are treating this as a high-risk
situation and preserving all legal options pending clarification.
```

## Citation Mode Requirements

### Mode A (Jurisdiction-Agnostic)

**Required:**
- Category-level references only
- No statute numbers
- Generic legal framework references

**Example:**
```
This may trigger obligations under federal child sexual exploitation
frameworks and state privacy and misappropriation doctrines.
```

### Mode B (Jurisdiction-Specific)

**Required:**
- Specific statute numbers (if overlay loaded)
- State-specific rules
- Verified citations

**Example:**
```
This may trigger obligations under 18 U.S.C. §2252 and K.S.A. §60-3301.
```
